## 🪵 Ode to the Golden Era of Internet Memes

In a world dominated by short-form videos and hyper-optimized social media algorithms, this project is a small, nostalgic tribute to the **Golden Era of the Internet**. 

Back in the late 2000s and early 2010s, internet culture wasn't about farming engagement or building personal brands. It was about pure, unadulterated nonsense. It was a digital Wild West where a simple hyperlink could lead you to a pop-up chaos of *Nyan Cat*, a majestic *Badger Badger Badger* loop, or the ultimate internet rite of passage: **The Rickroll**.

Rickrolling (`dQw4w9WgXcQ`) became the universal handshake of the web. It wasn't malicious; it was a harmless, shared inside joke between millions of strangers across forums like 4chan and Reddit. If you got tricked, you didn't get angry—you just smiled, bopped your head to the synth-pop intro, and immediately started thinking about how to trick your friends.

By mixing a modern Web3 crypto-scam aesthetic with the classic 2008 bait-and-switch, this project bridges the gap between today’s internet and the nostalgic, chaotic, and beautiful past we all secretly miss. 

Keep the tradition alive. Never gonna give it up.
